Goals and Funnels

Posted by John Hu on May 30, 2020

Introduction

As a second stage to my initial set-up of Google Analytics, I am now thinking about goals and the particular behaviors I want to track on this site. The goal I chose is visiting at least 5 different pages.

Process

My set up process of Goals was fairly straightforward. I began by navigating to Admin > Goals. I selected "New Goal" and chose Custom in step 1.

In step 2, I wrote the name of the goal (pages visited), setting the Type to "Pages/Screens per session". I then set it to greater than 5 to assign the goal that the user will need to visit at least 5 pages.

Presentation

Since the goal is not something that can be easily communicated to general users, I am treating it as an imaginary job, as if higher page counts will lead to greater revenue.

Luckily the basic design of the website is simple and clear enough that users can easily find and browse the other pages.

In an effort to polish the comprehensibility of the site, I replaced the lorem ipsum on the "About" page with a basic description so that any curious visitor may have a better context and greater likelihood to visit more pages.

Funnels

Prior to implementing the "pages visited" goal, I made destination goals for two of my posts. The only requirement was to visit those pages, and I set the funnel to "index.html", the home page.
